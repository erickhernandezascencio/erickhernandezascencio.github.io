This is a page where you can see all the courses that I have taken while at Stanford, my current courseload, and courses I plan to take in the future!

Current Coursework:

Spring 2019

CS 109
Probability for Computer Scientists. Probability course with a focus on machine learning applications in the last two weeks. Topics covered include: combinatorics, random variables, conditional probability, distributions, expectation, point estimation, and more.
Econ 1
Standard introductory macro/microeconomics class. Team based learning used for a flipped classroom style. Content covered includes: competitive markets, international trade, long term macroeconomic growth, and microeconomic fluctuations.
THINK 65
Thinking Matters program required of all freshman. This course focuses on the scenarios by which human extinction could occur within the next 100 years. Class content composed of academic reading, apocalyptic fiction, group discussion, writing, and role-playing.
CME 195
One unit intro to R course. Focuses on using R to import, format, visualize, and model data.

Past Courses:

Winter 2019

CS 103
Mathematical Foundtions of Computing. Built a foundation for proof-writing while also covering first-order logic, set theory, relations, functions, induction, finite automata, context-free grammars, Turing machines, decidable and recognizable languages, and more.
Math 53
Ordinary Differential Equations with Linear Algebra. Focused on the applications of second-order equations to oscillations, matrix exponentials, Laplace transforms, stability of non-linear systems and phase plane analysis, along with some numerical methods
PWR 1AF
Progrm in Writing and Rhetoric. An introductory writing class that is required of all freshman. This class in particular focused on the American education; touching on teaching pedagogy, segregation, learning disabilities, and more.

Fall 2018

CS 106B
Second course in the indroductory CS 106 series. Covers recursion,fundamental data structures (such as stacks, queues, sets) and recursive data structures (linked lists, trees, graphs).
Math 51
Linear Algebra and Multivariable Calculus. LinAlg portion covers orthogonality, linear independence, matrix algebra, and eigenvalues. Calculus portion covers unconstrained optimization via gradients, Hessians, constrained optimization, gradient descent and the multivariable Chain Rule
History 5C
Offered an interdisciplinary (legal, medical, historical) approach to understanding the extent and complexity of the global phenomenon of human trafficking. Focused on human rights violations and possible remedies.
